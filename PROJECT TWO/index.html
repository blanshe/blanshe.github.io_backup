The Rats of New York City

Is New York the most diverse city in the World? I don’t know. Maybe, yes, if you consider rats that thrive in this city! New York city administration has a data base of reported rat incidents since 2010. I explored the data set and plucked out interesting visuals.

By Musinguzi Blanshe

Published July 22, 2019

I had seen rats outside my apartment. But never cared. I picked interest in this subject when I was in Brooklyn and read 2020 District 14 District Needs Assessment report highlighting rats as top problem that need urgent attention. I immediately hit google and discovered that the New York Times and  OpenTheBooks.com, a nonprofit watchdog group had published an insight on the subject on May, 22, 2019.

But I said to myself, I must go for this data set and analyze it as I learn. Brooklyn has registered the highest of rat reported incidents followed by Manhattan, Bronx and Staten Island. Reported incidents in Brooklyn double incidents reported in Bronx.

Rat incidents are reported by dialing 311. The rats data set is updated everytime an incident is reported.

Let's look at Years!

The Times reported that New York Mayor, Mr. Bill de Blasio, calling for “more rat corpses,” unveiled a $32 million assault on rats in 2017, which included increased litter basket pickups, the deployment of solar-powered, trash compacting bins and rat-resistant steel cans. The city has also used dry ice to smother rats where they live."

But ironically, reported incidents never went down. Actually, the highest number of rat incidents was recorded in 2017 followed by 2016 and 2018 respectively. 2019 is not yer over. Let’s wait for the year to end.

But where exactly?

New York is a 306 square miles city. Rats can’t be everywhere. When sorted by zip codes—address—10026 has had the highest number of reports (5607) since city government started recording sight incidents. What is this zip code? I don’t know. I am not a New Yorker.
