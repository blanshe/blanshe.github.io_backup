Donald Trump won five counties for every county that Hilary Clinton won. Trump won 2,626 counties while Clinton took 487 of them nationwide. But Clinton won the popular vote by three million. But this not the big deal in USA politics—the deal is electoral college.

Trump's most significant support came from counties in the industrial Midwest where whites without a college education are the majority, according to analysis by the New York Times.

As U.S prepare for 2020, the big question in the Democratic Party is how to avoid 2016 debacle. The race for Democrat’s flag bearer has been often dominated by rhetoric of “I am the best candidate to defeat Trump.” But to defeat Trump, the party must recapture states such as Pennsylvania, Florida, Ohio, Wisconsin, Iowa and Michigan that Trump turned red in 2016.
