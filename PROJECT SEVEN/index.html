China's Money in Africa

In this project, I analyzed China’s money in Africa. I focused on the source of money key sectors where it has been invested in Africa and which countries have actually taken Chinese loans.

By MUSINGUZI BLANSHE

Published August 31st, 2019

My data source is China Africa Research Initiative based at John Hopkins University, New York.

The data was collected between 2000 and 2017. You can find the data here

Transport, power and mining top sectors where Chinese loans have been invested in Africa, according to a data base developed by China-Africa Research Initiative (CARI), think tank based at John Hopkins University.

CARI has developed a data-base tracking flow of Chinese loans to Africa and sector where these loans have been invested.

From 2000 to 2017, the Chinese government, banks and contractors extended US $143 billion in loans to African governments and their state-owned enterprises (SOEs), according to CARI report. Angola is the top recipient of Chinese loans, with $42.8 billion disbursed over 17 years.

Chinese President Xi Jinping, last year while opening Forum on China-Africa Cooperation (FOCAC), at Beijing’s Great Hall of the People, said China will invest more $60 billion in Africa. This will include $15 billion of aid, interest-free loans and concessional loans, a credit line of $20 billion, a $10 billion special fund for China-Africa development, and a $5 billion special fund for imports from Africa.

Source of Loans

The Exim Bank and China Development Bank have been the major source of loans invested in Africa.

Sources: China Africa Research Initiative

Key sectors where money is going

The CARI data-base shows a steady increase of loans invested in transport and power sector and fluctuations in loans invested in water, communications and mining sector.

Sources: China Africa Research Initiative

All sectors

But there is no record of money invested in sectors such as environment, population, good governance and food which are also critical for Africa’s economic transformation.
